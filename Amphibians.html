Amphibians

An amphibian (am·fi·bee·uhn) is a cold-blooded vertebrate animal that typically lives both in water and on land, undergoing metamorphosis from a larval stage with gills to an adult stage with lungs. Examples include frogs, salamanders, and newts.

Australian Green Tree Frog

The Australian Green Tree Frog is a large, bright green frog found in Australia and New Guinea. It is often seen around human dwellings and feeds on insects and spiders. It is known for its loud, distinctive croaking.

The green tree frog is considered one of the most popular pets for frogs. Due to their docile nature and long-life expectancy. This frog also has a strong resistance to disease.

Green and Golden Bell Frog

The Green and Golden Bell Frog is a strikingly coloured frog found in eastern Australia. It has green and golden-yellow markings and lives in marshes, ponds, and streams. This species is currently endangered due to habitat loss and disease.

The Green and Golden Bell frog consumes, spiders, crickets, butterflies and ants. They can also prey on other frogs by the sound of their call. This frog has also been known to consume froglets from their own species.

Bumpy Rocket Frog

The Bumpy Rocket Frog is a small, agile frog found in northern Australia. It has a bumpy, textured skin and is known for its powerful jumps. It inhabits a variety of wetland environments and breeds after heavy rains.

They are distinct and known for their loud voice which sounds like a high pitched “beep beep.” Which they use to call for mates.

Fun Fact

Frogs are the only remaining amphibians in Australia.
