Reptiles

A reptile (rep·tile) is a cold-blooded vertebrate animal that typically has scaly skin and lays eggs. Examples include snakes, lizards, turtles, and crocodiles.

Shingle-back

The Shingle back is a species of Skinks are a type of lizard found worldwide, with many species in Australia. They have smooth, shiny scales and often a streamlined body. Skinks are known for their ability to shed their tails to escape predators.

The Shingle-back can be found in all southern states except Tasmania in open grassland and woodland regions. It is also known as the two-headed lizard because it tail looks like a head, throwing predators off.

This Lizard diet mostly includes plants, berries and flowers. There are times where they will also eat snails, insects and even spiders. Although Yellow Blossoms and bright flowers are considered to be their favourite meals.

Australian Scrub Python

The Australian Scrub Python is the largest snake in Australia, growing to over 5 meters long. They are found in rainforests and woodlands of northern Queensland.

These reptiles are non-venomous and use their fangs to capture their prey. They are able to consume their dinner whole such as rodents, fruit bats, and possums. The scrub python is known for including juvenile kangaroos on their menu.

Fresh Water Crocodile

The Freshwater Crocodile is native to northern Australia inhabiting rivers, lakes, and billabongs. This crocodile is smaller and less aggressive than the saltwater crocodile.

The fresh water Crocodile will eat a wide range of prey which incudes spiders, fishes, frogs, turtles, lizards, snakes, birds and mammals. They use a “sit and wait method” to eat their prey where they lie motionless in typically shallow waters and wait for their prey to come close.

Fun fact

14% of the worlds reptile population is in Australia.
